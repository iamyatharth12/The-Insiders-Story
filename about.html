Studio · The Founder

Tanya Chachra

A boutique interior design practice founded on a single belief: a home is the most honest portrait of the people who live within it.

Trained in architecture and shaped by the craft traditions of India, Tanya Chachra has spent more than a decade designing residences for clients who see their homes not as backdrops, but as collaborators in the way they live.

Est. 2011 New Delhi 120+ Spaces
Tanya Chachra · New Delhi, 2024

Tanya grew up in a household where the home was a slow, ongoing conversation. Her mother, a weaver, would re-dye curtains with the seasons; her father, an architect, drew at the dining table every Sunday afternoon. The house was never finished — it was tended, like a garden.

She trained as an architect in Mumbai, then spent six years working alongside master craftspeople in Rajasthan, learning how a joint is cut, how a wall is plastered, how a room can be made to feel like a held breath. The Insiders' Story grew out of those years — out of a conviction that the smallest, slowest decisions are usually the most important.

The studio remains small by intention. Tanya is personally involved in every project, from the first conversation to the final styling, because she believes a home is too important to be delegated.

Design Philosophy

“I begin every project by listening — to the way a family moves, the way light enters in the afternoon, the small, almost unspoken things. The design is already in the room. My work is to reveal it.”

— Tanya Chachra

How we work

Four quiet commitments.

i.

Listen before we design

Every project begins with a long conversation — about the people, the rituals, the way afternoons are spent. The design that follows is in service of that conversation.

ii.

Choose by hand

Materials are not selected from a catalogue. We visit quarries, ateliers, and workshops. We hold every sample in real light, often over many weeks, before a single decision is made.

iii.

Prototype at full scale

A joinery detail is drawn full-size before it is built. A wall finish is mocked up in a corner of the room. Nothing is assumed.

iv.

Design for time

We choose materials and proportions that age beautifully — that will look as considered in twenty years as they do on the day we hand them over.

v.

Work with craftspeople

The studio collaborates with a small, trusted circle of carpenters, masons, weavers, and metalworkers — many of whom we have worked with for over a decade.

vi.

Stay involved

Tanya is personally present on every site, every week, for the duration of a project. The home we hand over is one we have lived inside with you.

The Name

The Insiders' Story.

The studio's name began as a small private joke. When Tanya was working on her first independent commission — a small apartment in South Delhi — a friend asked who the client was.

“I'm not sure I can tell you. They're an insider story.”

The phrase stayed. It captured something essential about the work: that a home is the most private story a person tells, and that designing one requires being trusted with that story, quietly, before a single wall is touched.

Fourteen years later, the name still means what it meant then. The studio is small. The projects are few. The clients become, over the course of many years, friends. And the work — the slow, careful, deeply personal work of designing a home — is treated, always, as the rare privilege it is.
