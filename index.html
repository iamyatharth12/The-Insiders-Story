01 — Manifesto

We do not design rooms. We write the first chapter of a life lived well.

The Insiders' Story is a boutique interior design practice founded on a single belief: a home is the most honest portrait of the people who live within it.

We work slowly, deliberately, and in close conversation with each client. Materials are chosen by hand, light is studied like a language, and proportion is treated as poetry. The result is interiors that feel inevitable — as if they had always existed, waiting quietly to be revealed.

From a quiet apartment in Delhi to a coastal retreat in Goa, our work is united not by a signature style, but by a quiet insistence on craft, calm, and the everyday rituals of dwelling.

“

I begin every project by listening — to the way a family moves, the way light enters in the afternoon, the small, almost unspoken things. The design is already in the room. My work is to reveal it.

— Tanya Chachra

02 — The Founder

Tanya Chachra

Founder & Principal Designer

Trained in architecture and deeply shaped by the craft traditions of India, Tanya founded The Insiders' Story after more than a decade of designing residences for clients who saw their homes not as backdrops, but as collaborators in the way they live.

Her practice is small by intention. She remains personally involved in every project, from the first conversation to the final styling — because she believes a home is too important to be delegated.

Case Study · Aaline

Aaline, in linen and light

A young family, a quiet street, and an apartment full of afternoon sun. The brief was simple — make it feel like a deep breath. We worked with undyed linen, lime-washed walls, and reclaimed oak to build a home that holds the warmth of the day long after it has set.

  • Challenge Bright south light in a small footprint.
  • Materiality Linen, lime plaster, reclaimed oak, travertine.
  • Result A home that softens the day.
Read the full case study

The Transformation

From layout to living

We opened the kitchen to the living room, lowered a single archway, and built a quiet window seat that catches the last hour of sun. Every detail was drawn by hand and prototyped at full scale.

Selected Materials

A palette of quiet things

Honed travertine from Rajasthan. Untreated linen in oat and chalk. Lime plaster mixed on site. Brass, oxidised by hand. Each material chosen to age beautifully, not to impress on the first visit.

04 — The Process

Four movements, one quiet composition.

See the full journey

  1. 01

    Discover

    Understanding the client's personality, daily rituals, and the unspoken architecture of how they live.

  2. 02

    Imagine

    Concepts, mood boards, hand sketches, and spatial narratives. We build a world around the brief.

  3. 03

    Create

    Detailed drawings, material samples, and a choreography of craftspeople. We choose by hand, prototype at full scale.

  4. 04

    Transform

    The final living experience — light, sound, scent, and texture composed into a space that feels inevitable.
