The Process

Four movements, one quiet composition.

Every The Insiders' Story project moves through four stages. Each is unhurried, deliberate, and built on a long conversation. From the first visit to the final styling, the studio stays personally involved.

Now
01 · Discover
02 · Imagine

—
01

Weeks 1 — 4

Discover

The studio begins every project with a long, unhurried conversation. We visit the home, walk the neighbourhood, and listen. We ask about the people who will live there, the rituals that shape a day, the small, almost unspoken things.

We photograph the space in every kind of light. We draw by hand. We collect fragments — a swatch of fabric, a corner of a tile, a piece of bark from the garden. The brief is written in collaboration with the client, and refined over many weeks.

  • VisitTwo on-site visits, including one at dusk.
  • InterviewA long, recorded conversation with the household.
  • BriefA written document we refine together.
02

Weeks 5 — 10

Imagine

From the brief, we build a world. Hand sketches become plans. Plans become sections. Sections become mood boards — collections of materials, light, and reference imagery that show, more clearly than words, the feeling we are working toward.

We present the concept in the client's home, often over a long lunch. We revise together. We argue gently. By the end of this stage, the project has a clear spatial narrative — a sense of how the rooms will unfold, what they will feel like at different hours, and how they will age.

  • SketchesHand-drawn plans, sections, and perspectives.
  • Mood boardsMaterial, light, and reference studies.
  • LayoutFinalised spatial narrative, signed off together.
03

Weeks 11 — 24

Create

The concept becomes a working drawing set. We visit quarries, ateliers, and workshops, often many times, to source and refine every material. We prototype at full scale — a joinery detail, a wall finish, a piece of furniture — in the studio or on site.

The site work begins. The studio coordinates a small, trusted team of craftspeople, and Tanya is personally present on site every week. Decisions that cannot be made on paper are made in the room, in real light, with the people who will live with the result.

  • DrawingsFull working set, including joinery and lighting.
  • SourcingHand-selection of every material.
  • BuildOn-site work, supervised personally by Tanya.
04

Weeks 25 — 36

Transform

The final stage is the most unhurried. We style the home with the household — flowers, books, the small, particular objects that make a room a home. We adjust the lighting for different times of day. We sit in the rooms, in silence, until the space feels inevitable.

When the project is handed over, it is not the end of the conversation. The studio returns at six months, a year, and beyond — to revisit the home, adjust the small things, and ensure the work continues to settle well over time.

  • StylingFinal layering of objects, art, and textiles.
  • HandoverA long walk-through, in every light.
  • StewardshipReturn visits, indefinitely, by arrangement.
